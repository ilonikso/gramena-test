Butter and fats

Butter is produced from milk fat, also known as milk cream. The cream is derived from raw milk through centrifugation which separates the cream from the milk. Churning the cream results in the fat molecules clumping together and creating the stiffer texture of butter. Butter can differ from a clear white to a more yellowish colour.

Butter and fats
  • Sweet cream butter
  • Lactic butter
  • Texturized butter
  • AMF (anhydrous milk fat)

Whey powders

Whey powder, or a derivative whey powder products, originate from the liquid residue that remains after cheese production. The different powders contain varying levels of proteins, lactose and minerals, and are commonly used in confectionery, bakery products, sports nutrition, soups and sauces.

Whey powders
  • Sweet whey powder
  • Demineralized whey powder
  • Whey powder concentrate
  • Whey powder isolate
  • Whey permeate
